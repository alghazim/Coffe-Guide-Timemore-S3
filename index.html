Timemore S3 — your grind settings

The S3 has 9 numbered positions with 10 clicks between each = 90 total settings, at 15 microns per click. For pour-over, start at click 60–65 and adjust. One click finer if sour; one click coarser if bitter.

Step by step
1

Boil & rest water 5 min before

Get water temperature right — this is non-negotiable.

Target 93–96°C for most roasts. No thermometer? Boil and wait 45 seconds — you'll land near 94°C. Light roasts benefit from the hotter end (95–96°C); dark roasts, 90–92°C.

💧 Filtered water with moderate mineral content (around 150 TDS) extracts significantly better than tap or distilled.
2

Grind fresh right before brewing

S3 at ~60–65 clicks · medium-fine · like coarse sand.

Grind your coffee immediately before brewing. Dial the S3's external ring to around position 6, 0–5 sub-clicks (≈ click 60–65 from zero). The S2C890 burrs produce an impressively even particle distribution here.

⚠️ The S3's external ring can shift accidentally — double check your setting before each grind, as it doesn't lock firmly into place.
3

Rinse the filter 30 sec

Remove papery taste and preheat your brewer.

Place your paper filter in the dripper and pour hot water through it to fully saturate it. This eliminates papery flavor and warms the vessel so brew temperature stays stable. Discard the rinse water.

ℹ️ Metal or cloth filters don't need rinsing — just preheat the brewer.
4

Bloom 0:00 – 0:45

Pour 40ml · 2× coffee weight · wait 45 sec.

Add your ground coffee and make a small well in the center. Pour 40ml in a slow spiral from center outward. This releases CO₂ trapped in the beans — fresh beans bubble vigorously. Rest 45 seconds undisturbed.

⭐ No bloom = stale beans. Buy beans roasted within the past 2 weeks.
5

First pour 0:45 – 1:15

Pour to 150ml total · slow spiral · stay central.

Starting at 0:45, pour in a steady spiral from center outward to 150ml. Keep the spout low — about 3–4cm above the bed. Avoid pouring on the filter walls.

⬇️ Pour low to minimize agitation. Aggressive pouring leads to uneven extraction.
6

Second pour 1:15 – 1:45

Pour to 225ml total · let drain slightly first.

Let the bed drain slightly, then pour a spiral bringing total to 225ml. The water level should stay just below the top of the grounds — never overflow the dripper.

7

Final pour 1:45 – 2:15

Pour to 300ml total · finish draw-down by 3:30.

Complete with a final pour to reach 300ml. Total draw-down should finish between 2:30 and 3:30. Past 4 minutes → go coarser. Under 2 minutes → go finer.

🕐 Target total brew time: 3:00–3:30. This is your key diagnostic number.

Troubleshooting

Tastes sour / sharp

Under-extracted. Go finer on the S3 (–1 to –2 clicks), increase water temp by 1–2°C, or slow your pour.

Tastes bitter / harsh

Over-extracted. Go coarser (+1 to +2 clicks), lower water temp slightly, or shorten brew time.

Tastes flat / dull

Stale coffee or water too cool. Buy beans roasted within 2 weeks. Increase water temp to 96°C.

Draining too fast / slow

Fast = go finer; aim for 3:00–3:30. Slow = go coarser — likely too fine or clogged filter.
